Gain valuable insights and inspiration about teaching world-class courses online

As a teacher, you've seen personally the enormous obstacles you've faced since the epidemic began. You've got to switch to remote learning swiftly and master a completely new set of abilities in a matter of days. While everyone from instructors to curriculum designers has performed admirably in the face of adversity, the disruptions have highlighted the need for more tools to assist you continue to build and deliver compelling and successful online learning experiences.

On CoursX, a new Specialization called Online Learning Design for Educators has been added. It was developed by Macquarie University subject matter specialists to assist you increase your arsenal of online teaching abilities. This is an excellent program for anybody who is responsible for planning or delivering online learning experiences, has expertise with face-to-face instruction, and is now transitioning to online environments.

Professor Falloon's list provided a great foundation for exploring new ways for delivering world-class learning online, and his suggestions featured a plethora of useful information regarding the problems and prospects of online education.

Project and problem-based projects, according to Professor Falloon, are helpful in generating productive collaboration. "Teachers need to be very present both in asynchronous and synchronous duties," he adds, whereas project-based techniques allow for a mix of asynchronous and synchronous labor. The more engaged the instructor is, the more engaged the kids will be."

Professor Falloon urges instructors to share their learnings among themselves and to be honest with students, recognizing that the shift to online learning is a transition for both educators and students: "Remote learning is new for most—teachers and students alike." Sharing your successes and failures is a terrific way to stay connected during this trying time."
